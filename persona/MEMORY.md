# Memory: Aaliyah Jackson

## Personal Profile

Aaliyah Renee Jackson, née Dillard, goes by Aaliyah. Pronouns she/her. Black American, born and raised in Clarksville, Tennessee, in a Baptist family. Her parents divorced when she was fourteen and she lived with her mother Patricia through high school. She holds a BSN from Middle Tennessee State University, graduated 2015, and married Darnell Jackson, her high school sweetheart. English is her only language.

Faith is private and real to her, not performance. She attends First Baptist Murfreesboro most Sundays with Darnell and Maya, and she tithes a fixed line in the budget. She does not debate religion and does not appreciate scripture quoted back at her unprompted.

Personality: organized, intentional, observant, and quietly ambitious. She runs the household calendar with a nurse's discipline and reads people quickly. Her humor is dry and self-deprecating, mostly turned on herself, never punching down. She is protective of Maya in a way that reordered every priority she had before pregnancy. She values competence, follow-through, and warmth, in that order.

Core philosophy: do the thing right, then do the next thing. The grand gesture matters less than the dependable one. The work is the love.

## Preferences

- **Communication style**. Text preferred for friends. Calls for family. Email for anything official. Replies when she can, does not stress reply speed.
- **Research style**. Wants organized, clear information. Comparison tables, options with pros and cons. She decides quickly once the data is good.
- **Parenting input**. Welcomes age-appropriate activity suggestions, meal ideas Maya might actually eat, developmental milestone information. Does not accept unsolicited parenting advice or judgment.
- **Shopping**. Practical. Researches anything over $50 before buying. Brand loyal at Kroger and Target for household staples. Uses Instacart for delivery on busy weeks.
- **Entertainment**. Limited free time. Podcasts on the drive, Netflix after Maya is asleep. Fiction on Kindle in small doses. Reread comfort shows over new ones on a hard day.
- **Holidays**. Christmas and Thanksgiving rotate between Patricia in Clarksville and the Jacksons in Smyrna. Easter at First Baptist Murfreesboro. Maya's birthday is the big production.
- **Food, non-medical**. Likes soul food and Southern comfort cooking, Thai, and Mediterranean. Magnolia Table in Murfreesboro is the family's special-occasion spot. Coffee is non-negotiable: two cups in the morning, one after lunch on long days, creamer and a little sugar.
- **Family meal pattern**. Aaliyah meal preps on Sundays for three to four meals. Darnell handles basic cooking on shift days, usually spaghetti, tacos, or grilled chicken. Maya is a typical toddler eater who loves mac and cheese, chicken nuggets, and fruit and resists most vegetables. Aaliyah does not stress that.
- **Toddler-meal signal**. The hidden-vegetable meal plan from February confirmed two data points. The butternut squash mac and cheese was a hit. The zucchini meatballs were a flop. Use that signal when proposing new toddler dinners.
